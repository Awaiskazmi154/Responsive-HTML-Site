A secret meeting in this small cottage changed the course of British history

Revolution House
Old Whittington, England

Revolution House offers visitors a friendly welcome and an introduction to the Revolution of 1688 which replaced James II with his daughter Mary and her Dutch husband William.

In the dark year 1688, rumour and unrest muttered up and down the country. Three local noblemen, the Earl of Devonshire, the Earl of Danby and Mr John D’Arcy, met in secret. Sheltering from the rain in the Cock and Pynot Alehouse, they began to make their plans to overthrow King James II in favour of his daughter Mary and her Dutch husband, William of Orange.

Travel by public transport

You can get to Revolution House by bus.
▪ The nearest bus stop is outside The Balls Heads. Buses 25 and 50 each run twice an hour from Chesterfield town centre. The bus stop is 0.1 miles / 0.2 km from Revolution House.
▪ The nearest train station is Chesterfield. The railways station is approximately 2.5 miles / 4 km from Revolution House.
▪ Chesterfield Coach Station, which is the National Express stop, is approximately 2.5 miles / 4 km from Revolution House.
▪ You can get a taxi with City Taxis (Tel: 01246 221111) or Carry Care (Tel: 01246 439118). Both have a wheelchair accessible vehicle.
